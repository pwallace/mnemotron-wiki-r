WHCL-FM 88.7

Overview

WHCL-FM 88.7 is the current Hamilton College campus radio station, broadcasting music, news, sports, and talk programming to the Mohawk Valley region. It is the successor to WHC, an earlier campus radio station that folded in spring 1948 due to lack of student interest — the closure documented explicitly in the March 5, 1948 issue of the Spectator.

Relevance to Research

The predecessor station WHC is documented in the Spectator corpus as having shut down in spring 1948 alongside the Canterbury Club (Episcopal student organization), both attributed to “lack of student interest.” The March 5, 1948 Spectator notes that WHC transmitter parts were still visible in the building at the time of the report. WHCL-FM 88.7 — the successor station — is confirmed as active and operational in the January 31, 2013 Spectator, where it was promoted on the front page with a promise of the semester’s schedule inside the issue. The precise founding date of WHCL as the replacement for WHC is not established in the synthesized corpus material, but its existence as a going concern into the 2010s is confirmed. The station is also noted in the Hamilton College Wikipedia entry as the current campus radio station.
