Black and Latino Student Union (BLSU)

Overview

The Black and Latino Student Union (BLSU) is a student organization at Hamilton College representing the interests of Black and Latino students on campus. It is documented in the Spectator corpus as an established and publicly active body by at least 1990, serving as a key voice in campus discussions of race, facilities equity, and student welfare.

Relevance to Research

The BLSU appears in the Spectator in connection with two major documented events. In March 1990, BLSU President Bridgette Burrowes ‘90 is named in coverage of the South Towers asbestos crisis, establishing the organization as an institutional advocate during a significant campus health emergency. In December 2013, the BLSU organized a candlelight service in the Hamilton College Chapel on December 11, 2013, following the death of Nelson Mandela — drawing an explicit line between the international struggle for racial justice and Hamilton’s own ongoing race dialogue. Africana Studies Assistant Professor Nigel Westmass spoke at the service, which was covered in the December 12, 2013 Spectator.
