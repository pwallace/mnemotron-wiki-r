John W. Chandler

Overview

John W. Chandler served as Hamilton College’s 15th president, announced in February 1968 at his first press conference. He came from Williams College, where he had been Dean of Faculty, and his presidency coincided with some of the most turbulent years of student protest and curricular reform in the college’s history. He departed in July 1973.

Relevance to Research

Chandler appears in the Spectator beginning with his announcement in the February 2, 1968 issue. His first documented act as president was presiding over the Board of Trustees’ decision to continue allowing military recruiters on campus despite faculty and student resolutions opposing it. Coverage extends through the Vietnam-era campus unrest of 1969–1972 and concludes with documentation of his July 1973 departure, which left the administration in a transitional state alongside the simultaneous exit of VP for Resources and Development Albert Wallace.

Notes

Role: 15th President of Hamilton College
Years active at Hamilton: 1968–1973
Key events: - Announced as 15th president at his first press conference, February 1968 - Oversaw Board of Trustees decision to continue military recruitment on campus despite faculty and student resolutions opposing it - Endorsed the Faculty Committee’s 4-1-4 curriculum reform in fall 1968, calling it “a splendid report — an imaginative response to the needs of Hamilton” - Presided over Vietnam-era campus upheaval including the May 1970 Spring Strike following Kent State - Formed an Advisory Committee on Drugs (1968) with students, faculty, and Dean Tolles - Departed July 1973, concurrent with VP Albert Wallace; J. Martin Carovano became acting president
