Obama at Hamilton (April 2025)

Overview

On April 3, 2025, former President Barack Obama spoke to approximately 5,200 people in the Margaret Bundy Scott Field House in conversation with President Steven Tepper — the largest single campus gathering documented in the full 78-year Spectator corpus. Obama explicitly addressed Trump administration threats to universities, and his visit catalyzed a sustained institutional response from President Tepper in the weeks that followed, including signing the AAC&U letter, delivering a public address naming trans, international, and undocumented community members, and coordinating Hamilton’s response with more than 500 institutions.

Key Points

  • Date: April 3, 2025
  • Venue: Margaret Bundy Scott Field House
  • Attendance: Approximately 5,200 people — the largest single campus gathering documented in the full 78-year Spectator corpus (surpassing the October 2013 Hillary Clinton lecture at 5,800 in the same venue, though the corpus characterizes the Obama visit as the largest; the discrepancy may reflect different counting or reporting methods)
  • Format: Obama in conversation with President Steven Tepper
  • Content: Obama explicitly addressed Trump administration threats to universities, describing such behavior as “contrary to the basic compact we have as Americans”
  • Campus response to Obama: Anonymous flyers criticizing Obama’s drone strike record were posted on campus prior to or during the event
  • Institutional response — Tepper actions in following weeks:
  • April 22: President Tepper signed the AAC&U (American Association of Colleges and Universities) letter, joining a collective institutional statement against federal overreach
  • April 29: Tepper delivered a public address explicitly naming trans, international, and undocumented community members — a notable act of institutional solidarity during a period of federal executive action
  • Tepper coordinated Hamilton’s response with more than 500 other institutions
  • Federal actions directly affecting Hamilton:
  • Termination of NSF grants affecting Professor Gibbons
  • Fulbright program disruptions affecting Hamilton students or faculty
  • NEH grant cuts affecting Professor Wilson
  • Executive orders removing Title IX protections for transgender students
  • Significance in the corpus: The Obama visit and its institutional aftermath represent the most sustained documented engagement between Hamilton’s leadership and a national political crisis in the extended corpus, exceeding even the 1970 Spring Strike in the breadth of institutional response documented
