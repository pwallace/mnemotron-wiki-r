Jodel Shooting Threat and Shelter-in-Place (April 2023)

Overview

On April 16, 2023, an anonymous post on the Jodel app prompted a campus shelter-in-place response involving approximately 20 law enforcement officers. The incident exposed critical failures in Hamilton’s emergency communication system and physical security infrastructure. An erroneous Everbridge “active shooter” alert was sent before any shooter was confirmed; classroom doors were found not to lock; and the administration denied Student Assembly President Nicole Soret’s request to cancel classes. Retrospective Spectator coverage documented lasting community trauma, making this the most significant campus safety crisis of the Wippman era.

Key Points

  • Date: April 16, 2023
  • Trigger: An anonymous post on the Jodel app (an anonymous social media platform) threatened a shooting on campus
  • Law enforcement response: Approximately 20 law enforcement officers responded to the campus
  • Erroneous alert: An erroneous Everbridge “active shooter” alert was sent to the campus community before any shooter was confirmed — a critical failure in emergency communication protocols
  • Physical security failure: KJ 102 (Kirner-Johnson Building, Room 102) doors were found not to lock, exposing a gap in the physical security infrastructure of a central academic building
  • Student governance response: Student Assembly President Nicole Soret requested class cancellation in the wake of the incident — the administration denied the request
  • Arrest: A student was arrested in connection with the threat
  • Lasting trauma: Retrospective Spectator coverage documented lasting trauma in the campus community; the incident had ongoing psychological effects beyond the immediate response
  • Systemic failures documented: The episode exposed gaps in emergency communication protocols, physical security infrastructure, and the administration’s responsiveness to student governance requests during crisis
  • Wippman era context: This was the most significant campus safety crisis of President David Wippman’s eight-year presidency (2016–2024)
  • Pattern: The Jodel threat occurred approximately two months after a separate campus security incident; the Spectator’s retrospective coverage treated the April 16 event as part of a larger pattern of institutional unpreparedness
