Alpha Delta Phi

Overview

Alpha Delta Phi is a Greek-letter fraternity founded at Hamilton College in 1832 by Samuel Eells and four other students, making it the oldest surviving Greek organization with roots at Hamilton. Its founding predates the Spectator corpus by over a century, but the chapter’s presence is documented throughout the archive, particularly in connection with the major institutional changes to Greek life in the 1990s and early 2000s.

Relevance to Research

Alpha Delta Phi appears in the Spectator corpus primarily in the context of disciplinary actions tied to Hamilton’s alcohol reform era. President Eugene Tobin suspended the chapter’s recognition in February 2000 following a Mardi Gras party that sent multiple students to the emergency room — one of the more serious documented alcohol incidents in the 1996–2003 period. The chapter was subsequently reinstated by Dean of Students Flossie Mitchell on October 28, 2003, documented in the November 7, 2003 Spectator. The chapter also lost exclusive control of its house under the 1995 Residential Life Decision, which required all Greek societies to integrate their houses into the college housing lottery. The chapter’s founding in 1832 is noted in the Wikipedia-sourced material as the earliest documented Greek founding at Hamilton.
