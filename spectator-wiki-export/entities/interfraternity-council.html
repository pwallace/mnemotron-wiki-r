Interfraternity Council (IFC)

Overview

The Interfraternity Council (IFC) is the governing body for Greek fraternities at Hamilton College. It adjudicates disputes between fraternities, sets alcohol policy for fraternity parties, administers the fraternity rush process, and coordinates the Greek system’s relationship with the college administration. The IFC is a persistent governance layer across the Spectator corpus from the late 1940s through the 1980s.

Relevance to Research

The IFC appears throughout the corpus in several significant contexts. In January 1953, it voted to formally rename “Hell Week” to “Initiation Week” and adopted five specific conduct rules constituting the first documented anti-hazing reform in the corpus. It co-sponsored student charity drives, including the 1957 Hungarian refugee work-week. In fall 1980, IFC president Rick Noble attended the “Mean Deans Fall Conference” alongside representatives from 16 colleges, responding to New York State’s newly amended Education Law prohibiting reckless hazing — though the IFC publicly asserted no Hamilton fraternities engaged in dangerous initiation rituals. The Council also coordinated responses to external pressures such as fraternity housing condemnations and alcohol policy changes. Its involvement in the 1963 Total Opportunity debate reflects its role in shaping who could access campus social life.
