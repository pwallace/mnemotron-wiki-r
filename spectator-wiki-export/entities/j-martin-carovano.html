J. Martin Carovano

Overview

J. Martin Carovano served first as acting president of Hamilton College following John W. Chandler’s July 1973 departure, then as regular president through the mid-to-late 1970s. His tenure bridged the tumultuous Vietnam era and the merged coeducational institution’s early years, and he is documented in the Spectator corpus as president as late as fall 1989.

Relevance to Research

Carovano appears in the Spectator from January 1974 onward, when coverage of Chandler’s departure and the withdrawal of chosen successor Joseph J. Sisco left Carovano in the presidency indefinitely alongside Eugene Lewis as Acting Provost. He is documented managing Hamilton’s capital campaign (with $6.35 million pledged by fall 1976) and the Chemistry building renovation. The September 1976 issue documents his status as regular president, and the 1981–1989 corpus continuation confirms his ongoing tenure into the late 1980s, covering major construction and pre-Residential Life Study tensions.

Notes

Role: Acting President, then President of Hamilton College (approximately 16th president)
Years active at Hamilton: 1973–approximately 1992
Key events: - Became acting president July 1973 following Chandler’s departure - January 1974: chosen successor Joseph J.
